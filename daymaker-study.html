Discovery:

As a team we did research on this problem and have had first hand experience with the lack of efficiency in clinical trials. What stuck out to us as the best starting point to solve the problem was tackling patient recruitment.

Most websites where you can sign up for clinical trials have very poor and outdated user experiences. You have to fill out massive forms with information that doesn’t necessarily qualify or disqualify you from a study and if you want to sign up for a different study you have to fill out a form with the same exact information all over again, wasting so much time.

Proposed Solution:

Create a patient on-boarding process that intelligently routes you to just the studies you qualify for and answering the questions you have to only one time.

Growth Machine:

The growth machine is something that is pushed by the 500 distro team heavily. The goal of it is to increase the key metric you are focusing on by experimenting as frequently and efficiently as possible in an organized fashion. Here’ a tl;dr version of the process:

  1. Brainstorm with your team.
  2. Comb ideas and score them by:
    1. Potential Impact
    2. Confidence in achieving the possible impact (I’ve seen someone do this before, I’ve done this before, etc.)
    3. Time/Money
  3. Choose the ideas with the best score until bandwidth is full for those responsible.
  4. After experiments have concluded interpret success and determine:
    1. Can we improve the experiment.
    2. Is it worth repeating / repeatable.
  5. Include your best repeatable experiments into your playbook.

Getting this process up and running takes some getting used to but once it has momentum its so effective. This process makes on-boarding new employees easy and creates wonderful transparency for the company, avoiding silos.
