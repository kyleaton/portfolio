Process (UX)

Problem:

Clinical trials aren’t meeting their deadlines. This wastes time, money and potential treatments for people in need.

Discovery:

As a team, we researched the stated problem and have also had first hand experience with the lack of efficiency in clinical trials professionally and personally. What struck us as the best starting point in solving this problem was addressing patient recruitment.

Most clinical trial websites have poor and outdated user experiences. Patients must complete extensive forms with information that doesn’t necessarily qualify or disqualify them from a study. Further, if patients want to sign up for a different study, they must re-submit additional forms with identical information. This wastes valuable time.

Proposed Solution:

Develop a patient on-boarding process that intelligently routes patients to multiple studies that are qualified for in a single, streamlined submission, without repeat questions and forms.

Using a combination of Google Analytics, Mixpanel and Inspectlet we are able to set up trackable success funnels and user segments. The MVP product along with a survey requesting feedback was sent to 300+ users. Those surveys in conjunction with one on one conversations with these participants led to improvements to the user experience.

Current Progress:

The product has been live now for over a year and has recruited hundreds of patients successfully 10x faster than the industry norm. The UX of the on-boarding piece of product has continued to evolve due to the user data collected through our third party applications.

Growth Machine:

The growth machine is something that is pushed by the 500 Startups team heavily. Its goal is to increase the key metric you are focusing on by experimenting as frequently and efficiently as possible in an organized and detailed fashion. Here’ a tl;dr version of the process:

  1. Brainstorm with your team.
  2. Comb through the ideas and score them by:
    1. Potential Impact
    2. Confidence in achieving the possible impact (I’ve seen someone do this before, I’ve done this before, etc...)
    3. Time & money spent to execute
  3. Select the ideas with the best score until the team's bandwidth full.
  4. After experiments have concluded, report success in improving the target metric and determine:
    1. Can we improve the experiment.
    2. Is it worth repeating or repeatable at all.
  5. Include your best repeatable experiments into your playbook.

Getting this process up and running takes some getting used to but once it has momentum its so effective. As you continue to run experiments your team will get better and better at scoring ideas and understanding their true potential output. This process makes on-boarding new employees easy and creates wonderful transparency for the company, avoiding silos.
