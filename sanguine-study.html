Intro to Sanguine

Sanguine is a biotech company and at the time of my employment was focused on improving in-home clinical trials in two ways: Recruit patients with digital marketing and developing technology to aid in collecting samples from patients in their homes.

My time at Sanguine was spent helping to create their mobile phlebotomy app Sangre and improving their patient sign up process.

Role: User Experience Manager

Main Responsibilities:
  • Wire-framing, user flow charts and low fidelity mocks.
  • Manage a small team of graphic designers.
  • Communicating functionality and features to developers.
  • Front end development (HTML, SCSS, Javascript).
  • Planning the infrastructure of and analyzing analytics.
  • Planning and designing A/B tests.
  • Collecting user feedback.

Development Process

At Sanguine I worked with a few different backend/full stack engineers both in house and external development shops. Mapping Event Tracking for Analytics:

After completing the design process I would mark up the designs signaling what events to track and how they will be represented in Google Analytics (e.g., category, action, and label). This step being crucial to measuring our key metrics and setting up A/B tests later on.

While The Product is Live

Monitoring Performance:

In order to track the progress of the product I would use the following methods:

  • Google Analytics to monitor acquisition success and user activity funnels.
  • Zendesk to open up a direct line of communication between me and the user. This allows me to not only diagnose problem areas but also ask user research questions normally reserved for surveys while they are interacting with product.
  • Inspectlet allows me to watch actual footage of the users experience along with heat/click maps. Being able to watch users interact with the product allows me see where users are having problems with the site.
  • Google Forms to take user surveys for qualitative data.
A/B Testing:

While monitoring the data and finding the problems areas in the funnel we would implement A/B tests in order to improve the user experience.
